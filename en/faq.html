📅 Lead times & booking

How far in advance should I book?
Ideally 2 to 4 weeks before the event for a standard shoot (conference, interview). For a live show or multi-site multiplex, plan 3 to 6 weeks since preparation is more demanding.
What are your turnaround times for urgent requests?
24 to 48h same-day is possible if the date is available. It has happened several times for agencies on last-minute replacements. Don't hesitate to call directly at +33 6 60 54 17 32.
How long does on-site setup take?
About 2h for the standard 3-camera 4K setup. For a corporate live show with set, allow 4h. For a multi-site multiplex, simultaneous setup on all sites before the event.

🌐 Internet connection & continuity

What happens if the internet connection drops during the live?
Continuity is part of the standard setup. I always work with two internet links in parallel: the venue's fiber + a dedicated 5G router. In case of failure on one link, the switch is automatic and invisible to viewers.
The venue's network is unreliable. Is that a problem?
No. I have several 5G routers that create a dedicated network independent from WiFi or local network. If 5G is unavailable (basement, dead zone), a satellite link can be set up on request.
Can you live stream from a location without fixed connection?
Yes. 5G router + dedicated broadcast SIM = standalone solution. For 5G-less zones, Starlink-type satellite link possible (to plan in the quote).

📡 Broadcasting & platforms

Can you broadcast on multiple platforms simultaneously?
Yes. I handle simultaneous broadcasting on YouTube, LinkedIn, X, Vimeo, Twitch, TikTok, Instagram Live, Zoom, Teams or private server. Adaptive multi-bitrate, each platform receives the optimal stream for its audience.
Is there a delay between different platforms?
Very small: 2 to 5 seconds depending on platforms (YouTube tends to be a bit slower than LinkedIn for example). Not noticeable for your viewers.
How do you configure broadcasting rights on LinkedIn Live?
LinkedIn Live requires prior verification of your company page. I handle RTMP/SRT configuration and technical compliance. You just provide the access.
Can you record simultaneously during a multi-platform live?
Yes. Local recording during the live, delivered the same day. So you have both the live broadcast AND the archived master file.
Which platform do you recommend for a B2B event?
LinkedIn Live for visibility with decision-makers, YouTube for reach and durable archiving, Zoom/Teams for internal events with interactivity. For an optimal mix: simultaneous multi-platform.

🎥 4K / Full HD quality

What's the difference between Full HD and 4K?
Full HD (1080p) = standard professional quality, perfect for live broadcasting and 95% of uses. 4K (2160p) = 4× more pixels, interesting for high-stakes corporate films, durable archiving, post-production reframing, or large-screen projections.
Is 4K essential for a professional result?
No. Full HD is largely sufficient for a live event. 4K makes a difference for: premium corporate films, long-term archiving, multiple post-production reframings, large screen projections. To discuss based on your intended use.
Can you broadcast in 4K live on YouTube?
Yes, YouTube accepts 4K live. But it consumes a lot of bandwidth (~30 Mbps upload) and only 5-10% of viewers benefit (most watch in 1080p or less). To evaluate based on your target audience.

🎬 Formats & positioning

What types of events do you film?
Any type of professional event with live broadcast: conferences, seminars, keynotes, hybrid AGM, product launches, roundtables, interviews, masterclasses, corporate talk shows, multi-site multiplex. Always B2B.
What's the difference between webcast, webinar and live streaming?
Webinar = fully online event, usually with laptop webcam and screen sharing (Zoom-style). Webcast = professional broadcast streaming of a physical event live on the web. Live streaming = generic term covering both. In B2B usage, all 3 are often used interchangeably, but my specialty is the webcast (TV broadcast quality).
What's the difference between filming and reportage?
Filming = recording an event "as is" from the control room, with live direction (multi-camera, live audio, integrated slides). Reportage = editorial post-production work with interviews, b-rolls, voice-over. I do broadcast filming, not reportage.
How does AGM filming work?
AGM = particular format (long, static, votes, remote speakers). I offer a specific setup: 2-3 fixed cameras + mobile camera in the room, integration of projected slides, electronic vote capture if needed, hybrid with remote shareholders via video.
Can you film without live streaming?
Yes, totally. Pure filming for archiving and replay = same setup, but without live broadcast. File delivered same day as usual. Similar price (sometimes slightly cheaper since no 5G router needed).
How many sites can you connect simultaneously in multiplex?
Up to 8 simultaneous sites. Multi-control-room architecture with vMix synchronized via broadcast-grade SRT/RIST. Beyond that, we enter very heavy setups that go beyond the scope of my standard services.
What's the maximum duration for a live show?
No technical limit. I've covered live shows of 4 consecutive days (GL Events case study). For long durations, we plan team relays and reinforced redundancies.
Which setup should I choose for my event?
Classic conference/seminar → Conference & seminar. Hybrid AGM or high-stakes launch → Corporate live show. Multiple simultaneous sites → Multi-site live streaming. Interview/roundtable → Interview & roundtable. Broadcasting on multiple channels in parallel → Multi-platform streaming (optional on everything). I can also guide you on request.

No. Nomacast is a lean structure specialized in event live streaming. Rather than an agency with multiple hierarchical levels (project manager, coordinator, technician), you have a single point of contact (Jérôme) who handles everything: pre-sales, site visits, technical setup, filming, delivery. It's faster, more direct, and the price reflects this (no agency layer to pay).

Yes, regularly. I work as an integrated technical provider for many audiovisual and event agencies (white-label, NDA possible, partner margins). See the Agencies page →
