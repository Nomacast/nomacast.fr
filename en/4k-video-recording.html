Definition

4K filming: when,
why, and how

4K Ultra HD (3840 × 2160 pixels) offers four times the resolution of Full HD 1080p. Applied to event filming, it produces an image of unmatched precision. But its value varies greatly depending on the intended use.

Nomacast films in 4K with Canon CR-N500 cameras, professional units designed for production. Motorised and controllable from the gallery, they combine 4K quality with operational discretion.

1080p vs 4K in practice

For live broadcast, Full HD 1080p remains the universal standard. Almost all platforms and viewers consume live in 1080p. Broadcasting in 4K doesn't improve the viewer experience in most cases and consumes 4 times the bandwidth.

The value of 4K is in recorded filming: the 4K file is exceptional raw material for post-production. Lossless reframing, stabilisation, export in multiple formats, projection on big screen or LED wall. 4K offers a flexibility you can't recreate later.

The Canon CR-N500 cameras

  • 4K/30p (and Full HD/60p) with professional autofocus, the image stays sharp even when speakers move
  • Extended dynamic range handles tricky contrasts well (bright background, dark scene)
  • Professional colorimetry natural and faithful rendering, compatible with post-production workflows
  • Remote control from the video gallery, no operator at each camera's foot
  • HDMI and IP output network-compatible for advanced workflows

Technical constraints of 4K

Storage: a 4K file at professional quality is 3 to 5 times larger than a 1080p file. For an 8-hour day, expect 400 to 600 GB. Real-time encoding: the video gallery must be powerful enough to encode 3 simultaneous 4K feeds without latency. Bandwidth: if you want to broadcast in 4K live, 25 to 40 Mbps of stable upload is required.

When to choose 4K

4K is recommended for premium event filming intended for post-production, projection on big screen or LED wall, long-term institutional archiving, and high-end corporate live shows. For pure live broadcast without later use, professional Full HD remains the optimal solution.
